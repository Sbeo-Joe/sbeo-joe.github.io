## Tariff War: New Weapon in US-China Strategic Competition

The US Treasury Secretary recently hinted at raising the baseline global tariff on Chinese goods from 10% to **15%**. While this may appear as simple trade policy, it's fundamentally a key weapon in US-China strategic competition.

The timing is significant. China has drastically lowered its growth targets, and the US is pursuing hawkish trade policies under a new administration. This is no coincidence.

> "This isn't trade competition—it's civilizational competition"

## Background: Why Now?

**First, supply chain security concerns.** Post-pandemic, America realized the critical importance of reducing Chinese dependency. Strategic goods like semiconductors, pharmaceuticals, and rare earths require tariffs as tools to decrease reliance.

**Second, domestic manufacturing revival.** Creating manufacturing jobs is politically vital. Tariffs make Chinese products expensive, giving US goods relative competitiveness.

**Third, maintaining technological dominance.** In semiconductors and AI, America must block Chinese technology acquisition. Tariffs and export controls are the tools.

**Fourth, strategic leverage.** While China weakens economically, US pressure gains negotiating advantage.

## America's Strategic Intent

Current US China policy transcends simple protectionism.

- **Economic containment**: Encircling China with allies. QUAD (US-Japan-India-Australia) and Indo-Pacific Economic Frameworks embody this.
- **Technology separation**: Cutting tech cooperation with China and restricting US investment in Chinese companies.
- **Alliance strengthening**: Deepening semiconductor supply partnerships with Korea, Japan, and Taiwan to exclude China.
- **Long-term positioning**: These measures lay groundwork for US-China competition victory over 5-10 years.

## Ripple Effects: Global Inflation and Supply Chain Restructuring

US tariff hikes are bad news for many nations.

**First, renewed global inflation risk.** Rising prices for cheap Chinese goods increase consumer prices, hitting low-income groups hard. Central banks finished rate hikes; tariff-induced inflation could reappear.

**Second, supply chain restructuring chaos.** Companies shift production to Vietnam, India, Thailand, causing short-term disruption. Transportation and capital investment costs rise.

**Third, retaliatory tariffs.** China and other nations will raise tariffs on US goods, deepening global protectionism.

## Impact on Korea

Korea sits at the center of this conflict.

- 📉 **Export headwinds**: Samsung, LG, Hyundai may face Chinese market access difficulties
- 🔌 **Supply chain diversification**: Korea must secure strategic supply sources as a "friendly nation"
- 💰 **Currency volatility**: Global uncertainty may intensify won weakness
- 🤝 **Strategic balance**: Difficult diplomatic positioning between US and China

## Historical Perspective: Lessons from the Past

Recall the 2018 Trump tariff war. The promised manufacturing job creation failed to materialize; instead, tariffs raised business costs. Farmers and consumers suffered most.

This situation differs because US-China competition transcends economics—it's become **civilizational rivalry**. Short-term economic efficiency yields to long-term strategic advantage as the priority.

## Key Takeaways

- ⚖️ US plans to raise China tariffs from 10% to 15% — deepening geopolitical competition beyond trade
- 🌍 Supply chain restructuring, inflation risks, and protectionism acceleration expected
- 🇰🇷 Korea faces difficult strategic choices between US and China; supply chain diversification and tech autonomy urgent
