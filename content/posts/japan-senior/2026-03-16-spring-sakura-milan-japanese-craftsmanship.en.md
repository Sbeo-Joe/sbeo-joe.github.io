Later this week, cherry blossoms will explode across Japan in a wave of color. At the same moment, Japanese craftsmen are completing their second consecutive appearance at Milan Design Week, where their fusion of delicate metalwork and Zen aesthetics left the world's design community breathless.

## Key Takeaways

- 🌸 Cherry blossoms are predicted to bloom in a rush across Japan starting this week, and elderly residents everywhere are looking forward to hanami — flower viewing
- Five Kawaguchi City metalworking companies appeared at Milan Design Week for the second consecutive year, captivating the world with "ZEN METAL GARDEN"
- Sensing the seasons and engaging with nature and culture is deeply connected to the ikigai and physical vitality of Japan's senior population

## 🌸 Sakura — The Symbol of Spring and Life in Japan

For older adults, *hanami* (flower viewing) is far more than recreation. It is a moment of **social participation**, **communion with nature**, and a full-body experience of life's renewal.

> "The cherry blossoms bloomed again this year. I was able to stay alive for another year. That alone makes me happy." — an <mark>82-year-old</mark> grandmother

## 🎌 Sakura and the Japanese Soul — A Bond More Than 1,000 Years Deep

Japan's deep attachment to the cherry blossom is inseparable from *mono no aware* (もののあわれ) — a tender, bittersweet appreciation for the transience of beautiful things.

| Cultural Meaning of Sakura | Content |
|---------------------------|---------|
| Meeting and parting | Blooms alongside school entrance ceremonies — symbol of new beginnings |
| The beauty of impermanence | The "graceful fall" of petals is central to Japanese aesthetics |
| Community bonds | Hanami gathers people together, strengthening local connection |
| Seasonal sensitivity | Embodies the Japanese attunement to nature's rhythms |

<span class="hl-blue">The capacity to feel a season turning, to let natural beauty move you — perhaps that itself is the mark of a rich and flourishing old age.</span>

## 🛠️ The Japanese Craftsmanship That Stunned Milan

Five metalworking firms from Kawaguchi City, Saitama Prefecture, presented "**ZEN METAL GARDEN**" — a fusion of exquisitely refined metal fabrication and native Zen aesthetics. Kawaguchi City has been a center of metalworking and casting for more than 400 years.

## 👴 Senior Craftspeople and the Art That Deepens With Age

One metal artisan, now 78, still enters his workshop every morning. "These tools are part of my body," he says. "Putting them down is unthinkable."

<span class="hl-blue">An elderly person with mastery is a living treasure for society.</span>

## 💡 Feeling Life in Spring, Taking Pride in Craft

Research consistently finds that seniors who carry a strong sense of *ikigai* have lower risk of heart disease, tend to experience delayed onset of dementia, and report higher day-to-day happiness.

This year's cherry blossoms will come a little early. Whether you find them in a city park or along a riverbank — breathe in this spring life as deeply as you can.

> "The cherry blossoms come back every year. So I want to stay healthy enough to come back to this spot every year too." — a <mark>79-year-old</mark> devoted hanami visitor
