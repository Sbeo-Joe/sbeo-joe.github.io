An <mark>80-something</mark> woman who pours hot water into her teapot every morning stays healthy because she has never stopped drinking tea — and science is beginning to prove her right.

## Key Takeaways

- Pharmacist-recommended Japanese tea practice has documented effects on <span class="hl-blue">promoting mental calm and longevity</span>
- Tea leaves typically discarded after brewing are rich in fiber and fat-soluble nutrients — reusable as rice toppings and porridge
- Green tea's **catechins** and **theanine** have been linked in research to <span class="hl-blue">dementia prevention and stress reduction</span>

## 🍵 Tea and the Japanese People——`2,000 Years` of Wellness Wisdom

The relationship between green tea and Japan runs extraordinarily deep. In the Kamakura period, Zen monk **Eisai** wrote that "tea is the ultimate medicine for nourishing life." More than <mark>800 years</mark> later, green tea remains a **cultural practice** that calms the mind and brings rhythm to daily life.

> "Tea is the greatest health practice the Japanese people have discovered over hundreds of years."

## 🔬 What Science Says

| Component | Primary Function | Benefits for Seniors |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Catechins** | Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory | <span class="hl-blue">Cardiovascular protection, immune support</span> |
| **Theanine** | Relaxation, concentration | <span class="hl-blue">Sleep quality, stress reduction</span> |
| **Caffeine** | Alertness, metabolic boost | <span class="hl-blue">Cognitive function support</span> |
| **Vitamin C** | Antioxidant | Skin health, immunity |
| **Fluoride** | Tooth enamel strengthening | <span class="hl-blue">Cavity prevention</span> |

A Tohoku University study found seniors who drink `4 or more cups daily` show significantly lower cognitive decline risk.

## ☕ The Pharmacist's Brewing Guide

- **`60–70°C` (low temperature)** → More theanine. Perfect before sleep or during rest
- **`80–90°C` (high temperature)** → More catechins. Better after meals

Seniors taking <span class="hl-red">iron deficiency medication</span> should avoid green tea for `30 minutes` before and after medication.

## ♻️ The Tea Leaves You've Been Throwing Away Are Nutritional Gold

- **Tea leaf furikake**: Stir-fry spent leaves with soy sauce, sesame, salt — sprinkle over rice
- **Tea leaf porridge**: Add spent leaves to rice while cooking for fiber and fragrance
- **Natural deodorizer**: Dried spent leaves absorb household odors
- **Bath soak**: Tea leaves in a cloth bag allow catechins to work gently on skin

This resonates with *mottainai* (もったいない) — the Japanese cultural concept of the tragedy of waste.

## 🌿 Korea and Japan — Two Tea Cultures

Korea has a rich tea tradition too, with Jeju Island green tea gaining international recognition. The act of carefully brewing tea is itself a practice of *mindfulness* — valuable for anyone navigating modern life.

## What One Cup Can Change

The daily habit of brewing tea carefully — appreciating its aroma, warming your hands on the cup — is simultaneously <span class="hl-blue">an investment in the body and a gift to the mind</span>. The wisdom of reusing tea leaves reflects a philosophy of living carefully and gratefully.
