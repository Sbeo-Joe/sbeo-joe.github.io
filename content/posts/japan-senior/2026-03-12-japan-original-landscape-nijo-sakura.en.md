## 🏔️ Japan's Original Landscapes — Why They Matter Now

Travel media outlet TRiP EDiTOR's ranking of "Japan's Original Landscapes TOP30" has sparked widespread interest, as travelers seek out the country's most unspoiled and emotionally resonant scenery.

*Genpukei* (原風景) refers to landscapes etched deep in collective memory — nostalgic, beautiful, and profoundly moving. Terraced rice fields, mountain villages, fishing hamlets, and historic townscapes — many were lost during Japan's rapid economic growth, but some endure.

| Landscape Type | Character | Notable Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Terraced paddies | Rice fields cascading down mountainsides | Noto, Echigo |
| *Satoyama* | Villages where humans and nature coexist | Kyoto Miyama, Shirakawa-go |
| Fishing villages | Tiny communities by the sea | Ine, boathouse villages |
| Historic streets | Edo-to-Showa architecture preserved | Narai-juku, Kakunodate |

> "The original landscapes quietly teach us what we lost in our pursuit of convenience."

The growing number of senior travelers visiting these places isn't mere nostalgia. It's a <span class="hl-blue">deep desire to see "truly beautiful things"</span> in the second half of life.

## 🌸 Nijo Castle SAKURA NIGHTS — Immersion in the Edo Era

Starting <mark>March 19</mark>, Kyoto's World Heritage **Nijo Castle** hosts "Nijo Castle 2026 SAKURA NIGHTS."

This isn't a typical light-up event. It offers <span class="hl-blue">full immersion into the Edo period</span>. The castle's grand architecture and cherry blossoms are illuminated to create the sensation of stepping through time into the Tokugawa era.

Built in <mark>1603</mark> by Tokugawa Ieyasu, Nijo Castle is where the *taisei hokan* (大政奉還 — the historic return of political power to the Emperor) took place. Cherry blossoms blooming in this solemn space represent the very essence of Japanese beauty.

## 🎌 Japan's Beauty, Recognized Worldwide

Japan's original landscapes and traditional architecture have earned global admiration. "When you go to Japan, visit the countryside" has become common advice among international travelers.

This respect extends beyond tourism — it's reverence for the <span class="hl-blue">wisdom of coexisting with nature</span>. *Satoyama* ecosystems, terraced paddy irrigation techniques, and wooden architecture all embody the Japanese aesthetic and philosophy of nature.

## 💡 Lessons for All of Us

In our fast, convenient lives, we forget to simply look — slowly, quietly. Original landscapes remind us of the richness found in pausing to gaze at the world around us.

This spring, why not take a walk through a nearby mountain village?
