"My back hurts because I'm getting older — nothing to be done." If that's what you believe, it's time to reconsider. The real cause of the lower back pain affecting <mark>80%</mark> of the Japanese population is, surprisingly, neither posture nor age — as the latest medical research is now revealing.

## Key Takeaways

- About `80%` of Japanese people experience lower back pain; rates are especially high among those over 60
- Most lower back pain is not directly caused by poor posture or aging — <span class="hl-red">psychological and social factors</span> are deeply involved
- The worst thing you can do for chronic back pain is rest — inactivity makes it worse

## 🔍 "85% of Back Pain Has No Identifiable Cause" — A Remarkable Medical Fact

For decades, lower back pain was understood as a problem of discs and bone deterioration. But current medicine recognizes that <span class="hl-red">`approximately 85%` of back pain is "non-specific" — no structural cause can be found</span>, even with MRI.

| Type of Back Pain | Proportion | Main Cause |
|------------------|------------|------------|
| Non-specific back pain | ~85% | Stress, poor sleep, anxiety — psychological factors |
| Lumbar disc herniation | ~4% | Disc degeneration or protrusion |
| Lumbar spinal stenosis | ~5% | Narrowing of the spinal canal |
| Other specific back pain | ~6% | Fractures, infection, cancer, etc. |

## 😰 The Pain Amplifier — How Stress Worsens Back Pain

The most important new insight is that <span class="hl-red">psychological stress literally amplifies back pain</span>.

The brain is the organ that processes pain. Under chronic stress, the brain's pain-processing system becomes hypersensitive — what might normally register as mild tension starts being experienced as intense pain. This is called **central sensitization** (*chūsūsei kansa*).

For Japanese seniors, **post-retirement stress** is a particular concern. Losing the structure and identity provided by work can trigger chronic anxiety and loneliness — a hidden driver of persistent back pain.

## 🚶 The Truth That Changed Everything: Rest Makes It Worse

Once, the standard treatment for back pain was rest. Modern medicine has completely reversed this.

**Rest is back pain's enemy.** When you stop moving, the muscles supporting the spine weaken, and pain persists longer and more severely. Western back pain guidelines now recommend <span class="hl-blue">staying as active as possible</span> even for acute pain (like a thrown-out back).

- 🔑 **Keep moving gently, even when it hurts** — within comfortable range
- 🔑 **Walking is the safest and most effective back pain intervention**
- 🔑 **Water walking reduces joint stress — ideal for seniors**
- 🔑 **Core muscle strengthening is the best long-term prevention**

## 🌸 The "Endure It" Culture — Why Japanese Back Pain Becomes Chronic

Japan has a deep cultural tendency to endure pain stoically. Among older generations especially, "it's not worth going to the doctor" and "it's just age" lead many people to leave pain untreated.

But this endurance culture <span class="hl-red">accelerates chronic pain development</span>. When early back pain is ignored, the brain learns and encodes a "pain pattern," making chronicity far more likely.

Seeking expert help — whether physical therapy or cognitive behavioral therapy for pain — is not weakness. It's wisdom.

> "Not 'nothing to be done because I'm old' — but 'because I'm older, I'll take better care.'"

Back pain is genuinely improvable with the right knowledge and action.
