"I do not even drink alcohol — and my doctor says I have fatty liver." It is a comment heard with increasing frequency in Japanese medical clinics. The progression specialists now call the "domino effect" — <span class="hl-red">fatty liver → diabetes → chronic kidney disease</span> — is emerging as one of Japan's most serious hidden health crises among older adults.

## Key Takeaways

- 🔑 Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is surging among people who never drink
- 🔑 The fatty liver → insulin resistance → diabetes → chronic kidney disease chain has been medically documented
- 🔑 Japan has approximately 13.3 million chronic kidney disease patients — roughly one in three seniors is at risk

## 🔬 How Fatty Liver Leads to Kidney Failure

When fat accumulates in the liver, the organ's ability to **process insulin** deteriorates. As insulin effectiveness declines, blood sugar levels stay chronically elevated — **insulin resistance** — which progresses to diabetes. Sustained high blood sugar then slowly damages the kidneys' capillaries, eroding their filtering capacity. This is <span class="hl-red">diabetic nephropathy</span> — the leading cause of dialysis in Japan.

| Stage | Condition | Risk |
|-------|-----------|------|
| ① Fatty Liver | Fat accumulation in liver | Onset of insulin resistance |
| ② Fatty Hepatitis | Inflammation added | Diabetes risk 3x+ |
| ③ Cirrhosis | Liver tissue fibrotic | Liver cancer and kidney failure risk |
| ④ Chronic Kidney Disease | Kidney function below 30% | Dialysis or transplant may be needed |

## 🧓 The "Thin Fatty Liver" Trap

"**Thin fatty liver**" — fat accumulation despite normal body weight — is especially common in <mark>women over 65</mark>. Age-related muscle loss (*sarcopenia*) shrinks the body's sugar-burning capacity, and the surplus gets stored as liver fat. This explains why many seniors say they are eating less but gaining weight or seeing rising blood sugar.

> "Fatty liver is a silent disease. It advances without symptoms, and by the time it is noticed, the kidneys may already be damaged." — Gastroenterologist

## 🌸 Ancient Wisdom, Modern Trap

Japan has a centuries-old proverb: ***hara hachi bu*** — "eat until 80 percent full." Okinawa's centenarians practice eating to 70 percent capacity, consistent with modern research on caloric restriction and longevity. But today's seniors face a different threat: the transformation of Japan's food supply toward processed carbohydrates and convenience foods is eroding the metabolic advantages traditional Japanese cuisine once provided.

## 💪 Three Habits to Protect Your Liver and Kidneys

- <span class="hl-blue">**Habit 1: Walk 10 minutes after meals**</span> — Blunts post-meal blood sugar spikes and reduces liver fat storage.
- <span class="hl-blue">**Habit 2: Cut back on fruit and juice**</span> — Fructose is converted directly to fat by the liver; fruit juices are a leading dietary driver of fatty liver.
- <span class="hl-blue">**Habit 3: Annual abdominal ultrasound**</span> — Blood tests alone often miss fatty liver. For anyone over 60, adding an abdominal ultrasound to the annual health check is strongly recommended.

## What This Means for Us

<span class="hl-blue">Reviving the ancient wisdom of *hara hachi bu* for a modern diet</span> may be the simplest single path to protecting both liver and kidneys simultaneously. The choices on today's dinner plate are already writing the story of the kidneys ten years from now.
