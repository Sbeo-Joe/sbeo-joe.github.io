"Maybe I've been forgetting things a bit more lately" — millions of older adults in Japan carry this quiet worry. An app developed specifically for this moment has just received the <span class="hl-blue">Asia Health and Longevity Innovation Award</span> and launched for public use.

## Key Takeaways

- 📱 'PiCA,' a cognitive function testing app developed with a national university medical school, has officially launched after receiving the Asia Health and Longevity Innovation Award
- Seniors can now perform self-assessments of cognitive function with a smartphone, opening the door to earlier detection and response
- By lowering the threshold for seeking care, PiCA empowers older adults to become active agents in managing their own health

## 📱 What Is PiCA?

"**PiCA**" is a cognitive function testing application co-developed with the medical faculty of a national university. Using a smartphone or tablet, it allows users to assess memory, attention, and judgment easily and at home — without a clinic visit.

> "Early detection is everything in dementia care. Once symptoms are advanced, the options available become far more limited." — a medical specialist

## 🧠 Why Early Detection of Dementia Is Critical

<span class="hl-red">Research indicates that the brain changes associated with Alzheimer's disease can begin 10 to 20 years before symptoms appear.</span>

- **Drug therapy effectiveness**: At the MCI stage, medication and lifestyle modification can measurably slow progression
- **Non-pharmacological interventions**: Cognitive training and exercise therapy are most powerful when applied early
- **Preserving autonomy**: The period during which a person can independently manage finances and medical decisions is extended

## 🌐 Japan's Smart Care Vision

<span class="hl-blue">When seniors harness technology, they can live longer and maintain independence further into life</span> — this is the defining aspiration of Japan's smart care movement.

## 🎌 Japan's Preventive Health Culture Meets Technology

Japan has a concept known as *mibyo* (未病) — a state between full health and illness, drawing on Eastern medicine's emphasis on intervening before disease fully manifests. PiCA is precisely the kind of tool built to detect and address this window.

## 💡 From Waiting to Taking Charge

<span class="hl-blue">PiCA's deeper message is that older adults need not be passive recipients of care — they can become informed, empowered stewards of their own cognitive health.</span>

If the thought "I've been a little more forgetful lately" has crossed your mind, do not simply chalk it up to aging. The tools to act on that feeling are now in your pocket.
