<mark>94 years old.</mark> One lung surgically removed. One ear nearly deaf. Yet actor Ohmura Kon smiles and says: "No problem." Behind that lightness lies a career of more than `70 years` facing age head-on — and a philosophy of life that speaks to every generation.

## Key Takeaways

- Despite major physical limitations, Ohmura Kon continues to perform as an active actor at <mark>94</mark>
- In conversation with geriatric medicine authority Dr. Hideki Wada, he shares his concrete approach to "aging without fear"
- Both "laughter" and "having a purpose" are proven medically — and Ohmura embodies both

## 🎭 A Living Legend: Who Is Ohmura Kon?

Born in 1931, Ohmura Kon is a comedian and actor who has been performing since Japan's earliest television era. Past 90, he still appears in stage productions and on screen.

In GOETHE magazine's conversation with Dr. Hideki Wada, Ohmura's approach to physical decline was laid bare — and what surprised the doctor wasn't the severity of his conditions, but how utterly unbothered Ohmura seemed by them.

> "Even with one lung, even with one ear that won't hear — I can still do what I want to do. That's enough, isn't it?"

## 🏥 Living with One Lung — A Philosophy of Loss

<span class="hl-red">The lungs are vital to life — they sustain every breath.</span> Losing one dramatically reduces respiratory capacity, making physical exertion and projecting one's voice far harder. For an actor, the voice is everything. Losing the organ that powers it would end most careers.

But Ohmura's response was different: "Work with what's left." This is the secret of his longevity and continued activity.

In Japanese geriatric medicine, *frailty prevention* (フレイル予防) is a major focus. <span class="hl-red">Once frailty sets in, the risk of needing care rises sharply.</span> The best tools against frailty? Social engagement and having a sense of purpose. Ohmura has lived by both for his entire career.

## 😄 Laughter as Medicine — What Science Confirms

| Health Benefit of Laughter | Mechanism |
|---------------------------|-----------|
| Immune boost | Activates NK (natural killer) cells |
| Stress reduction | Lowers cortisol (stress hormone) |
| Better circulation | Improves blood flow throughout body |
| Pain relief | Triggers endorphin release |
| Dementia prevention | Stimulates brain, promotes social connection |

A research team in Osaka found that people who laugh daily have roughly `40% lower risk` of needing long-term care than those who rarely laugh.

<span class="hl-blue">Perhaps those who make others laugh live longer than those who simply receive the laughter.</span>

## 🧠 Dr. Wada's Message: The Right Way to Accept Aging

Dr. Hideki Wada is one of Japan's foremost geriatric medicine experts. His bestselling book *The 80-Year-Old Wall* is credited with reshaping how Japan thinks about elderly healthcare.

His key message in the Ohmura conversation: most elderly people experience physical decline as "loss" and become depressed. Ohmura's approach is the opposite — focus not on what's been lost, but on using fully what remains.

This is medically sound. <span class="hl-blue">Maximizing residual function</span> is the key to maintaining quality of life in aging. And Japan's tendency to over-restrict elderly people — "don't eat this," "don't do that" — often strips them of vitality unnecessarily.

## 🌸 Five Lessons from Ohmura Kon

- 🔑 **Focus on what you can do, not what you can't**: Direct energy toward remaining abilities
- 🔑 **Keep a job or a role**: Social connection sustains mental and physical vitality
- 🔑 **Build laughter into daily life**: It boosts immunity and melts stress
- 🔑 **Make peace with aging — don't fight it**: Age is not an enemy
- 🔑 **Stop comparing**: Don't measure yourself against your younger self or others; just give your best today

> "Don't fear growing old. Even as you age, there are still things you can do. Searching for them — that is what it means to be alive."

Ohmura Kon's smile says it all.
