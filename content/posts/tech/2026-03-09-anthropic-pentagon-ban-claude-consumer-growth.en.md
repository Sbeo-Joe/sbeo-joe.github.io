Blacklisted by the Pentagon, the Claude app's new install count surpassed ChatGPT immediately after. The crisis became marketing.

## Key Takeaways

- The <mark>DoD</mark> officially designated Anthropic as a supply chain risk and canceled its contract, warning of federal contract risks for startups
- Both Microsoft and Google officially announced that Claude access **remains guaranteed** for all non-DoD customers
- Claude app daily active users (DAU) and new installs surpassed ChatGPT for the first time, with explosive consumer market growth

## The Full Story of the Pentagon Split

The conflict began with the collapse of contract negotiations between Anthropic and the U.S. Department of Defense. Both sides could not narrow their differences over the scope of military use and AI safety standards, and the DoD ultimately officially designated Anthropic as a <span class="hl-red">supply chain risk vendor</span>. This effectively means a blacklist in the U.S. federal procurement ecosystem.

Anthropicstated there were conditions it could not negotiate under its safety principles regarding military use of AI. This was not a simple contract failure, but a collision between AI ethics and national security interests. Anthropic is reportedly also considering legal action.

## Cloud Vendors Draw the Line

When news of the Pentagon split spread, **Microsoft** and **Google**, which provide Claude on their cloud platforms, immediately issued official statements. Both companies clarified that "all enterprise customers except DoD projects can use Claude normally." <span class="hl-blue">Claude accessibility in the enterprise cloud market remains completely unchanged</span> was the core message.

This reflects the structural nature of the cloud industry. Anthropic contracting directly with the DoD and using Claude via Microsoft Azure or Google Cloud APIs are separate contractual relationships. Cloud vendors operate their own government contract channels separately, and they drew a clear line that the current situation has no impact on general enterprise customers.

## Paradoxical Growth: Crisis Creates Brand Recognition

The most notable metric came from the consumer market. After the DoD situation, Claude app <mark>new installs</mark> and daily active users surpassed ChatGPT for the first time in history. The controversy created an unexpected marketing effect, imprinting Claude on general users worldwide.

> "Claude's consumer growth surge continues after Pentagon deal debacle. New installs are growing faster than ChatGPT."

This is being interpreted as a case study showing that an AI company's "ethical stance" can actually build consumer trust. Particularly among developers and younger users opposed to AI militarization and weaponization, Anthropic's standing has been strengthened.

## Lessons for Startups

This situation starkly exposed the risks when AI startups pursue federal government, especially DoD contracts. Federal procurement contracts are large and stable, but once designated as a supply chain risk, trust across the federal ecosystem is lost. Yet the paradox was also proven: brand image in the private market can actually improve.

How AI companies find the balance between safety principles and growth is a turning point that the entire industry must watch.
