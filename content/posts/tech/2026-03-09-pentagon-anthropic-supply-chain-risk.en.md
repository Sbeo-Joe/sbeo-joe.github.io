The U.S. Department of Defense has officially designated AI startup **Anthropic** as a "Supply Chain Risk" entity. President Trump publicly mocked Anthropic, saying he "fired them like dogs," and CEO **Dario Amodei** immediately declared legal action, saying he had "no choice."

## Key Takeaways

- DoD officially designates Anthropic as a "supply chain risk" — a major administrative action that could exclude the company from government procurement
- Paradoxically, **Anthropic**'s Claude AI is currently <span class="hl-red">actively deployed in U.S. military operations in the Iran conflict</span>
- Microsoft states "Anthropic products will continue to be available to customers," limiting immediate impact

## What Is a Supply Chain Risk Designation?

The DoD's "Supply Chain Risk" designation is not a mere warning. It is an official administrative action that provides legal grounds for the DoD to restrict or terminate contracts and procurement with the designated entity. This is the same mechanism previously applied to Chinese telecom companies Huawei and ZTE.

Anthropicwas founded in <mark>2021</mark> and has been competing at the frontier of the U.S. AI industry. The **Claude** series has spread rapidly among enterprise and government customers, and has been widely used in defense and intelligence contexts. This designation directly collides with that trajectory.

## The Irony: Designated a Risk While Being Used in Operations

The most striking aspect of this situation is its contradiction. According to CNBC reporting, at the very moment the DoD designated Anthropic as a supply chain risk, <span class="hl-red">Claude AI was actively being used in U.S. military operations in the Iran conflict</span>.

> "We have no choice but to fight this legally." — Dario Amodei, Anthropic CEO

President Trump used social media to publicly mock Anthropic, saying he "fired them like dogs." AI lobbying groups have criticized the Trump administration for "undermining its own AI agenda."

## Industry Impact: Microsoft as Buffer

Microsoft immediately responded: its official statement that "Anthropic products will continue to be provided to existing customers" plays an important buffering role. Microsoft provides Claude through Azure cloud, and this action alone does not immediately terminate those contracts.

Meanwhile, Wired reported another angle: despite OpenAI officially prohibiting military use, the Pentagon has reportedly been testing OpenAI models in Iran operations through Microsoft channels. The gap between AI companies' stated policies and actual government use is widening.

## Outlook

This situation signals that the relationship between the AI industry and the U.S. federal government has entered a new phase. <span class="hl-blue">If Anthropic wins in court</span>, the designation could be invalidated and set a precedent constraining similar future government actions. <span class="hl-red">If it loses</span>, Anthropic's position in the federal procurement market will be significantly weakened.

As AI companies accelerate their push for defense contracts, how the dual status of "government partner" and "supply chain risk" is resolved will be a central concern for the entire industry.
