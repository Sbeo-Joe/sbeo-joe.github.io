`GPT-5.4` has arrived. <span class="hl-blue">The first OpenAI model with native computer use capabilities</span>, it signals the start of the autonomous agent era — AI that can independently operate a mouse and keyboard.

## Key Takeaways

- `GPT-5.4` is the first OpenAI model with **native computer use** built in — direct screen control without separate tooling
- Available in two versions — **Pro** for coding, spreadsheets, and presentations; **Thinking** for deep reasoning
- On launch day OpenAI signed a contract with the U.S. Department of Defense — a striking contrast to Anthropic being blacklisted the same day

## What Makes GPT-5.4 Different

OpenAI described `GPT-5.4` as "the most powerful and efficient frontier model for expert-level work." Where its predecessor `GPT-5.2` focused on professional tasks, `GPT-5.4` adds **autonomous agent** capabilities on top.

The standout feature is <span class="hl-blue">native computer use</span>. Previous AI models only processed what users typed; `GPT-5.4` can perform mouse clicks, keyboard inputs, and screen scrolling at the operating system level. It is the equivalent of Anthropic's Claude natively incorporating the `computer_use` capability that was previously offered only as a separate API.

Spreadsheet and presentation generation has also improved significantly. According to Engadget, `GPT-5.4` produces presentations with stronger aesthetic range than previous models. Deeper integration with Excel, PowerPoint, and other Office tools will substantially expand the scope of knowledge worker task automation.

## Pro vs. Thinking — The Two Versions

| Version | Primary Use | Characteristics |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| **Pro** | Coding, data analysis, document work | Speed and efficiency focused, specialist task optimized |
| **Thinking** | Complex reasoning, science and math | Enhanced step-by-step reasoning, accuracy first |

This bifurcated strategy mirrors Anthropic's Claude 3.5 (intelligence) vs. Claude 3 Haiku (speed) and Google's Gemini 2.0 Pro vs. Flash structure. The major AI players are converging on a two-track strategy of high performance and lightweight efficiency.

> "OpenAI's most powerful and efficient frontier model" — OpenAI official announcement

## Industry Impact

The launch of `GPT-5.4` directly disrupts the entire AI coding agent market. On the same day, Cursor announced **Automations** — an autonomous agent coding automation feature that lets a Slack message or timer automatically trigger an AI agent to make code changes. Combined with `GPT-5.4`'s computer control capabilities, the automation level of software development pipelines takes a step up.

The RPA (Robotic Process Automation) market is also directly in the blast radius. <span class="hl-red">UiPath, Automation Anywhere</span> and other established RPA vendors now face an AI agent that threatens to cannibalize their core value proposition — computers controllable by natural language instruction without separate flow design.

## Outlook

The practical deployment of AI agents that directly control computers has the potential to transform enterprise workflows within `6–12 months`. But ethical and security concerns are also emerging. If AI can autonomously control a computer, privilege misuse and security breach risks represent new threat vectors. Public information about what safeguards OpenAI has built remains limited. How the industry establishes trustworthiness and governance for autonomous AI agents is the challenge the entire sector must solve.
