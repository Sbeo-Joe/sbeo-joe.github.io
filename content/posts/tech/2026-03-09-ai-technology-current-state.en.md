## The Challenges Facing AI Technology

AI technology is advancing rapidly, but real-world deployment continues to surface significant limitations.

### The Limits of AI Translation

AI is being used to translate Guernésiais (a Norman language spoken in Guernsey), but the results can be wrong. According to BBC reporting, AI-powered automatic translation remains unreliable for minority languages, demanding careful human oversight.

### The Limits of Robotic Recruiters

AI-based automated recruitment systems are being deployed in the UK for care worker hiring. But a critical question arises: **can a robot genuinely assess human qualities?** For roles where empathy and interpersonal skills are paramount — such as caregiving — the limits of AI recruitment systems are becoming painfully apparent.

## The Evolution of AI Architecture

On the technical frontier, a new concept is attracting significant attention: **"Agentic Engineering Patterns."** This is a methodology for designing AI systems that operate autonomously — moving beyond simple question-and-answer models to automate complex, multi-step workflows.

## Conclusion: Balancing Technology and Trust

AI is undeniably powerful, but rigorous validation is essential depending on the domain of application. Particularly **in areas where human judgment is critical, AI should be treated as an assistive tool only**.
