Apple has fired the opening shot of a <span class="hl-blue">premium strategy pivot</span>. Just after widening its market base with the budget MacBook Neo, reports are now emerging that Apple is simultaneously preparing three top-tier "Ultra" brand products. Alongside this, manufacturing innovation experiments using <mark>3D-printed aluminum</mark> for iPhone and Apple Watch have also been confirmed.

## Key Takeaways

- At least three top-tier "Ultra" lineup products in simultaneous development immediately after MacBook Neo launch
- Exploring introduction of <span class="hl-blue">3D-printed aluminum</span> for iPhone and Apple Watch — targeting both manufacturing cost reduction and design freedom
- Low-end to premium two-track strategy aimed at capturing market share across all price points

## The Ultra Lineup — What Changes

According to The Verge, Apple is developing three top-tier "Ultra" brand products that go beyond the existing Mac Air and Pro lines. These products will feature the **M5 Ultra chip** and reportedly include entirely new form factors targeting creators, AI engineers, and 3D rendering workstation users. Differentiating positioning from the existing Mac Studio and Mac Pro will be central.

This is interpreted as Apple moving to more aggressively pressure **Dell XPS and Microsoft Surface Studio** in the premium market, while staking out ground in markets requiring premium AI performance. As AI inference workloads are exploding, demand for high-performance local computing is surging.

## 3D-Printed iPhone — Bloomberg-Reported Manufacturing Innovation

According to Engadget citing Bloomberg reporting, Apple is exploring introducing <span class="hl-blue">metal 3D printing (DMLS: Direct Metal Laser Sintering)</span> technology to the aluminum casings of iPhone and Apple Watch. Applying this technology — currently used mainly in aerospace and medical fields — to consumer electronics would be an industry first.

Three expected benefits: first, **mold cost reduction** compared to conventional CNC machining; second, improved heat dissipation and weight reduction through complex internal structures that conventional machining cannot produce; third, supply chain flexibility for small-batch, high-variety production. However, the key challenge is whether current metal 3D printing surface quality can meet Apple's standards. Apple products require surface roughness of <mark>Ra 0.1μm or below</mark> achieved through CNC machining.

## The Logic of the Two-Track Strategy

| Product | Positioning | Purpose |
|---------|------------|--------|
| MacBook Neo | Entry-level | Attracting Android/Windows users |
| MacBook Air/Pro | Core | Maintaining key revenue source |
| Ultra lineup | Top-tier | Maximizing premium margins, handling AI workloads |

> "Open the ecosystem door with low price, maximize revenue with premium — Apple's formula never changes."

If Apple's premium counterattack leads to actual launches combined with 3D printing manufacturing innovation, it could simultaneously achieve supply chain efficiency and product differentiation. The industry expects the Ultra lineup announcement to come at <mark>WWDC 2026</mark> in the second half of the year or at the fall event.
