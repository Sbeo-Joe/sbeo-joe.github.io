## Correction Signals in Tech Stocks

U.S. macroeconomic analysts are sounding the alarm: **"The AI bubble is bursting."**

### Signs of Excessive Valuation

- **Gap between earnings and share prices**: AI companies with unclear profitability command inflated valuations
- **Bubble deflation signals**: Analysts at MacroBusiness and others are pointing to the AI bubble becoming a reality
- **Shift in investor sentiment**: Focus moves from pure technological innovation to **actual profitability**

### Deteriorating Macro Environment

Simultaneously, **shocks to the Australian economy** (recession fears driven by energy and interest rate pressures) are weighing negatively on technology investment sentiment globally.

## Recalibrating Investment Strategy

In this environment, investors are reviewing:

1. **Reducing exposure to overvalued companies**
2. **Selectively identifying AI firms with genuine profitability**
3. **Using the correction phase to accumulate positions**

## Conclusion

The long-term value of AI technology is undeniable, but **a short-term correction phase appears necessary**. Savvy investors should view this period as **an opportunity to build long-term positions**.
