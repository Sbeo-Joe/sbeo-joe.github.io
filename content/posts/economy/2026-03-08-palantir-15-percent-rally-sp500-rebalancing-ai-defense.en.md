CNBC notes a double tailwind: first, Anthropic's designation opens up defense contracts for Palantir to fill. Second, the war itself means accelerating defense AI budgets.

The S&P 500 rebalancing formalized a structural shift: AI infrastructure companies — the physical layer that makes AI run — are entering the benchmark index.

- **Vertiv**: Power and cooling solutions for AI data centers. The critical infrastructure pick-and-shovel play on the AI buildout
- **Lumentum**: Optical components for ultra-high-speed data center networking. Direct beneficiary of exploding AI interconnect demand
- **Coherent**: Photonics technology pushing the speed limits of AI computing with optical solutions
- **EchoStar**: Satellite communications enabling global AI infrastructure connectivity

<span class="hl-blue">Index inclusion triggers automatic buying from trillions in passive index funds</span> — a mechanical tailwind that independent of sentiment.

## Investment Implications

The market is showing a clear bifurcation: defense AI and infrastructure stocks are surging while the broader index hits lows. This is classic "crisis differentiation" — capital flowing toward companies that benefit from exactly the conditions causing broader market pain.

AI infrastructure has a durable demand thesis regardless of geopolitics: data center investment scales superlinearly with AI model size, and power, cooling, and optical networking remain in structural undersupply. The risk: <span class="hl-red">if oil stays above $100, data center operating costs rise sharply</span> — a direct headwind for the same AI infrastructure story.

As long as geopolitical uncertainty persists, capital rotation into defense AI and cybersecurity is likely to continue. Whether this trend is structurally durable beyond the Iran war resolution is the defining portfolio question for H2 2026.
