The Iran war has moved beyond the Middle East to batter global financial markets. International crude breached <mark>$110 a barrel</mark> and Seoul's Kospi dropped more than <mark>8%</mark>, triggering an intraday trading halt in an unprecedented sequence of circuit-breaker events.

## Key Takeaways

- WTI crude surpasses <mark>$110/barrel</mark> — highest since the Russia-Ukraine war shock of 2022
- Seoul Kospi falls <mark>-8%</mark>, sidecar and circuit breaker triggered in succession
- Japan's Nikkei <mark>-6.7%</mark>, U.S. futures slide in tandem — Asia-wide panic

## When the Strait of Hormuz Shakes, the World Stops

According to BBC reporting, the swift escalation of the Iran war has raised serious fears of disruption to Middle East energy supply chains, pushing oil above <mark>$110 a barrel</mark> — the highest level in roughly four years, since the surge that accompanied Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022. The key risk analysts are watching is <span class="hl-red">the possibility of a Hormuz Strait blockade</span>. Roughly 20% of global crude shipments pass through the strait; if it closes, warnings are mounting that oil could spike above <mark>$150</mark>.

The U.S. government's order for personnel to evacuate the Saudi Arabia embassy reads as a crisis signal that this conflict has moved beyond a bilateral Iran-Israel clash toward a <span class="hl-red">regional all-out war</span>. Should Iran launch large-scale retaliation following strikes on its nuclear facilities, a scenario in which the entire Gulf energy infrastructure comes under threat cannot be ruled out.

## Asia's Domino Collapse

South Korea's Kospi plunged more than <mark>8%</mark> in morning trade, prompting an intraday trading halt. According to Yonhap News, a sidecar was triggered first, followed by a full circuit breaker — an extremely rare occurrence on the same session. The episode laid bare the structural vulnerability of an economy as heavily dependent on energy imports as South Korea's. The won-dollar exchange rate spiked simultaneously, roiling the foreign-exchange market as well.

Japan's Nikkei also cratered <mark>-6.7%</mark>, while S&P 500 and Dow Jones futures fell sharply across the board. Crypto markets — Bitcoin, Ethereum — sold off in tandem; only gold and the dollar found buyers as safe havens.

> "An energy supply-chain breakdown is not simply an oil price problem. It sends cascading shocks through manufacturing costs, logistics costs, and consumer prices across the board."

## Inflation Rekindled vs. Recession — The Fed's Dilemma

<mark>$110</mark> oil imposes a dual threat on the global economy simultaneously. First: <span class="hl-red">reflation</span>. If an energy-price spike coincides precisely with the moment the Fed is trying to enter a rate-cut cycle, the central bank may be forced to pivot back to tightening. Second: <span class="hl-red">growth shock</span>. Rising energy costs directly compress corporate margins while simultaneously eroding consumer spending power. Business Insider analysis notes that warnings are already emerging that a significant portion of the U.S. economy has entered recession territory — making the compound effect of an oil shock all the more alarming.

## Transmission Channels

| Wave | Impact |
|------|--------|
| 1st (Immediate) | Energy and defense stocks surge / airlines, logistics, manufacturing plunge |
| 2nd (1–3 months) | Consumer price inflation, margin compression, rate-path reassessment |
| 3rd (Long term) | Surge in energy-security investment, accelerated renewable transition, supply-chain diversification |

**Beneficiaries**: Energy, defense, gold
<span class="hl-red">Losers</span>: Airlines, logistics, autos, retail, growth stocks

The next two inflection points for this crisis are clear: whether the Iran conflict escalates further, and the outcome of the <mark>March FOMC</mark> meeting. If surging oil snuffs out rate-cut expectations, downside pressure on equities will intensify considerably. Every investor on the planet is watching the Middle East front and Washington at the same time.
