SpaceX is weighing a Nasdaq listing that sources describe as potentially the largest IPO in US history — and in the same week, Nasdaq announced a partnership with crypto exchange Kraken to launch <mark>24/7</mark> tokenized stock trading. Together, these developments signal that the structure of capital markets is about to change more fundamentally than at any point in the past three decades.

## Key Takeaways

- SpaceX is actively exploring a Nasdaq listing and early S&P 500 index entry, according to Reuters sources — a deal that would dwarf previous record IPOs given the company's valuation
- Nasdaq and Kraken have announced a formal partnership to launch <mark>24/7</mark> tokenized stock trading, allowing equities to trade like cryptocurrencies — continuously, without market-hours restrictions
- The convergence of SpaceX's potential IPO and Nasdaq's crypto-equity integration represents a structural transformation of how capital forms and trades in the US market

## 🚀 SpaceX: The IPO That Would Rewrite Records

SpaceX has been the most valuable private company in the world for several years. Its last disclosed private valuation reached <mark>$350 billion</mark>, making it larger than most publicly traded companies in the S&P 500. A public listing at or near that valuation would exceed Alibaba's 2014 IPO (<mark>$25 billion</mark> raised) by an order of magnitude.

The strategic logic of a listing is clear. SpaceX's capital requirements are enormous: Starship development, Starlink satellite constellation expansion, and Mars mission infrastructure all require continued multi-billion-dollar investments. Public markets offer a more liquid, lower-cost capital source than private rounds — and early index inclusion would create structural buying pressure from index funds that must hold all S&P 500 constituents.

The choice of Nasdaq over NYSE is notable. Nasdaq has become the default exchange for technology-first companies — Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, and Nvidia all list there. SpaceX on Nasdaq would reinforce the exchange's dominance in the most valuable sector of US equity markets.

However, the timeline and structure remain uncertain. Elon Musk has historically resisted public market accountability — he took Tesla private briefly, created significant disclosure controversy, and has stated he prefers the operational freedom of private ownership. A SpaceX IPO would subject the company to quarterly earnings scrutiny, SEC disclosure requirements, and activist investor pressure that have not characterized its private existence.

## 📊 Nasdaq + Kraken: 24/7 Markets Are Coming

The more structurally transformative story may be the smaller headline. Nasdaq's partnership with Kraken — one of the largest and most compliance-focused crypto exchanges — to enable <mark>24/7</mark> tokenized stock trading represents a fundamental redesign of equity market mechanics.

Currently, US stock markets operate <mark>9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET</mark>, Monday through Friday. Pre-market and after-hours trading exist but are illiquid. The system was designed around physical trading floors and settlement cycles from an earlier era. Tokenized stocks — blockchain-based representations of equity ownership — can trade continuously, settle instantly, and be held in wallets rather than brokerage accounts.

| Feature | Traditional Equity | Tokenized Equity |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Trading hours | 9:30AM–4PM ET, weekdays | 24/7, 365 days |
| Settlement | T+1 (next day) | Near-instant |
| Custody | Brokerage account | Crypto wallet |
| Fractional ownership | Limited | Native |
| Global access | Restricted by jurisdiction | Programmable |

The Nasdaq-Kraken partnership is the first major institutional signal that traditional finance is willing to adopt crypto market infrastructure — not just "blockchain technology" in the abstract, but the actual settlement and custody mechanics of decentralized systems.

## ⚖️ Winners and Losers

<span class="hl-blue">Nasdaq wins</span> on both fronts: a SpaceX listing would be the most-watched market event since the dot-com era, generating enormous trading volume and brand prestige. The Kraken partnership positions Nasdaq to capture the tokenized equity market before NYSE or any foreign exchange.

<span class="hl-blue">Retail investors globally</span> win if 24/7 tokenized trading succeeds — access to US equities outside US market hours is currently expensive and inefficient for international investors. Tokenization removes many of those barriers.

<span class="hl-red">Traditional brokerages</span> face structural pressure. If equities can be held in crypto wallets and traded peer-to-peer on 24/7 infrastructure, the custody and transaction fee models of legacy brokerages are challenged.

<span class="hl-red">For SpaceX</span>, going public is a double-edged development. Capital access and liquidity are clear benefits. But public market accountability, quarterly earnings guidance, and activist investor dynamics are constraints that have historically complicated operations at mission-driven companies.

## 🔭 The Long View

The convergence of these two stories — a potential record IPO and the infrastructure for 24/7 equity trading — reflects a broader truth: capital markets are catching up to the operational reality of a global, 24-hour economy. Companies like SpaceX operate across time zones. Their employees and customers span every continent. Restricting capital formation to a 6.5-hour New York window is an anachronism.

> "Nasdaq is building the market infrastructure for a world where the biggest company in the index might be a rocket company — and where stocks trade like Bitcoin."

The speed of this transition depends on regulatory clarity. The SEC has been cautious about tokenized securities, and the legal framework for blockchain-based equity ownership remains incomplete. What Nasdaq's institutional endorsement does is make it significantly harder for regulators to treat tokenized equities as a fringe category.
